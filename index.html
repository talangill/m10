Client Presentation (Group + Individual)

5 min pitch + 5 min Q&A · Week 8 · Smart casual / business attire

5-minute pitch structure

  1. Opening hook (~30 sec) — compelling question, quote, or statistic
  2. Context & opportunity (~1 min) — strategic problem, key stakeholders
  3. The concept (~2 min) — what it is, why best, research support, key visuals
  4. Implementation plan (~1 min) — changes, who's involved, timeline / Service Blueprint
  5. Costs & benefits (~1 min) — environmental, social, financial impacts + 3-year forecast
  6. Risks & success measurement (~1 min) — KPIs, mitigation strategies

How the Calculation Works

3-step process — each step is input for the next

Step 1 — Word Count: The Business Case max is 4,000 words. Every 3% excess deducts 0.1 from your oral score first.

Step 2 — Business Case multiplier: Score 2 → final grade 1.0, oral denied. Score 4 → ×0.8. Score 6 → no change. Score 8 → ×1.1 (max 10). Score 10 → ×1.2 (max 10).

Step 3 — Individual Contribution: Score 4 → −1.0. Score 6 → no change. Score 8 → +0.5 (max 10). Score 10 → +1.0 (max 10).

Assessment Rubrics

Understand exactly how you'll be scored. All oral criteria are graded 2/4/6/8/10 and averaged.

🗣

Oral Assessment Rubric

6 criteria · scores averaged for final oral grade

Criterion 10 Excellent 6 Sufficient 4 Insufficient
1. Empathize
Needs & perspectives of clients		 Insights clearly articulated and actionable; depth in identifying pain points Insights articulated but may lack depth or actionability Insights unclear or not actionable; empathy not used effectively
2. Define
Problem statement		 Specific, focused, actionable problem statement; coherent narrative; root causes identified Clear problem statement but may lack specificity; structured but may miss some patterns Vague or not actionable; no coherent narrative; unstructured
3. Ideate
Ideas & brainstorming		 Variety of creative solutions; clear brainstorming methodology; strategic selection aligned with FDV criteria Some creative solutions; limited methodology; partial FDV alignment Few or no creative solutions; no methodology; no FDV alignment
4. Prototype
Tangible representations		 Well-detailed, purpose-driven prototypes; iterates meaningfully based on feedback Prototypes developed but lack detail; some iteration but not fully incorporating feedback Poorly developed or not purpose-driven; no iteration; no openness to improvement
5. Test
Systematic feedback collection		 Structured methods; results clearly communicated; feedback integrated into final concept Collects feedback but lacks systematic methods; results communicated but lack clarity No systematic feedback collection; unclear results; no integration
6. Integration
Coherent process + iteration		 All steps logically connected; iteration used to the fullest extent Some connections between steps; aware of iteration value but didn't fully iterate Unable to show how steps connect; not aware of iteration value
